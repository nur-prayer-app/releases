Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2, 2026

Nur is a prayer tracking app built for Muslims. Your privacy matters, and this policy explains what data Nur collects, how it is used, and what control you have over it. We keep things simple and honest — no legalese.

What Nur collects

  • Prayer tracking data — the prayers, fasting days, and qadaa goals you log inside the app.
  • Email address — only if you create an account for cloud sync. Used solely for authentication.
  • Google account info — if you sign in with Google OAuth, we receive your name and email from Google. We do not access your contacts, calendar, or any other Google data.

That is everything. Nur does not collect device identifiers, IP-based location, usage patterns, or any other telemetry.

How your data is stored

By default, all your data lives on your device in localStorage. Nothing leaves your browser or desktop app unless you choose to enable cloud sync.

If you turn on cloud sync, your prayer data is stored in Supabase (a hosted PostgreSQL service). The data is tied to your account and is not shared with anyone.

Analytics

Nur does not currently include any analytics. In the future we plan to add Plausible Analytics, a privacy-friendly, cookie-free analytics tool that does not track individual users and is fully compliant with GDPR, CCPA, and PECR. No personal data will be collected through analytics.

Third-party services

  • Supabase — cloud database for optional sync. Your data is stored securely and is not shared with third parties.
  • Google OAuth — used for sign-in only. We request the minimum scope (email and profile).

Nur does not sell, rent, or share your data with anyone. There are no ads, no ad networks, and no data brokers involved.

Cookies and local storage

Nur uses localStorage to save your prayer data, preferences, and settings on your device. Nur does not use tracking cookies. If Plausible Analytics is added in the future, it will operate without cookies.

Your control over your data

  • You can export all your data as a JSON file from Settings at any time.
  • You can delete your account and all cloud-synced data at any time from within the app.
  • If you never enable cloud sync, your data never leaves your device.

Children

Nur does not knowingly collect data from children under 13. The app does not require an account to function, and no personal information is collected without explicit action (signing up for cloud sync).

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted here with a new date. We will not reduce your rights under this policy without clear notice.

Contact

Questions or concerns about your privacy? Reach out to us.
